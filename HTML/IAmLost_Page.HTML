  1. Take the Pet to a Vet or Animal Shelter to Check for a Microchip

    2. If the pet doesn't have a visible ID tag, take them to a local veterinarian or animal shelter to scan for a microchip. This is often the quickest way to find the owner, as the chip may contain up-to-date contact information.

  2. Document the Pet's Appearance

    3. Take clear photos of the pet, showing their full body and any unique markings. Note their breed (or best guess), size, color, and any distinct features like scars, spots.

  3. Post on This and Other Lost and Found Platforms

    4. Spread the word by posting on community platforms. Include the pet's photo, description, and where they were found.

  4. Keep the Pet Safe

    5. If you're able, temporarily shelter the pet in your home, and provide them with water, food. Be cautious when interacting with the pet, as they may be scared or anxious. If you're unable to care for them, place them in our shelter.
